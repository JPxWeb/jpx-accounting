## 1. Executive summary

The largest immediate upside is defensive and cheap: four verified trust-breakers — demo seed lines polluting every production report, VAT figure and SIE export; fabricated-but-approvable OCR fields; 41 known dependency vulnerabilities (8 HIGH in next, 20 in hono, several on live security gates); and a wrong statutory rule live in the advisor corpus — are all S/M-effort fixes sitting directly under the product's "verifiable, compliance-first" claim. The clearest offensive window is externally timed: Fortnox's Access/BLINK moves triggered a verified accountant-trust crisis in March 2026, making SIE opening-balance import (L4) plus the already-locked free accountant seat the acquisition wedge, with a verified empty pricing slot (~399–799 SEK/mo) between the software and service bands. "AI suggestions" are now commoditized — even the human-gate pattern is copied — so differentiation must shift to what only JPx has: answers dual-grounded in regulation and the user's own hash-chained ledger, suggestions that learn from review decisions, and provenance made visible in normal mode. Bank truth is the binding constraint on the advisory thesis (cash-runway is systematically wrong on a purchase-only ledger); the judged cheapest path is CSV statement import first, with an honesty label in the interim. Regulatory dates are mostly runway (e-invoicing inquiry 2027-11-30; ViDA 2030-07-01) with one exception: EU AI Act Article 50 applies **2026-08-02** and needs a documented assessment plus machine-readable marking now.
